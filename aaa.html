Bullet Comment of 2017: Xi (囍)
Xi is not a common standard Chinese character. In traditional folk symbols, it means that you have obtained a title and got married, and two happy events happened one after another, which is "double happiness." Later, Xi was used to express marriage. In the year of 2017, bullet comment Xi appeared 218,062 times, distributed in tens of thousands of videos, and appeared in scenes such as reunions of couples in anime.
Bullet Comment of 2018: True (真实)
In the year of 2018, bullet comment True appeared 477,065 times out of more than billions of bullet comments.
Bullet Comment of 2019: Ah I'm dead (awsl)
Awsl is the abbreviation of Ah I'm dead in Chinese. If showed how the audience feel happiness, joy, and excitement when they see their favorite content in videos. In the year of 2019, out of a total of more than 1.4 billion bullet comments, the annual bullet comment "awsl was sent 3,296,443 times.
Bullet Comment of 2020: Ye Qing Hui (爷青回)
Ye Qing Hui is the abbreviation of "the youth of an elder comes back." No matter what age the users were born in, when the familiar melodies from their memory were played again, the familiar pictures flashed in their mind. In 2020, a total of 2,275,216,512 bullet comments appeared, and Ye Qing Hui was sent 5,420,359 times.
Bullet Comment of 2021: Po Fang Le (破防了)
Po Fang Le means the defense is broken. While it originally means physical damage to break through a defense, in the context Bilibili, it often indicates that the psychological defense is broken, especially in the year of 2021 together with several major social changes. The total amount of bullet comments in Bilibili exceeded 10 billion in 2021.
